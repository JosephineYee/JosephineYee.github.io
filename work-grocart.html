Grocart - A Mobile App for Grocery Shopping

Overview

The Grocart mobile app serves as a UX challenge I devised to hone my thinking and design abilities. It aims to facilitate convenient grocery shopping for consumers.

Role and Responsibility
  • UI/UX Designer
  • Wireframing
  • Prototoyping
  • Visual Design
Tool Used
  • Figma
Scenario

I work as a UX designer for a startup that is developing a new mobile grocery delivery application, with Instacart as one of our competitors.

Task

Develop a user flow outlining the complete process of initiating and submitting a grocery delivery order, which includes selecting a nearby store, adding items to the cart, proceeding to checkout, and selecting a delivery time.

User flow

In order to anticipate the consumer's journey, I created user flows for selecting a store, adding products to the cart, proceeding to checkout, and choosing a delivery time. Additionally, I included steps for adding alternative payment options and repurchasing products.

Wireframe

I typically begin my design process with low-fidelity wireframes. This approach allows me to structure the design by positioning images, text, buttons, and icons.

Screens

UI Design

Rationale

Here is the reasoning behind my design choices for the interface:

  • I utilized a green and yellow palette to symbolize freshness and optimism.
  • A drop-down menu was implemented for selecting from a large list, ensuring a clean layout and easy access to selections.
  • Additionally, a darker color was employed for the call-to-action (CTA) to enhance user attraction.

    • Here's what I've learned from this challenge:

    I found great satisfaction in working on this grocery app. This challenge highlighted the importance of comprehending the user's objectives and prioritizing them throughout the process, leading to a more effective analysis of the flow. While the primary focus was on ideation and user flow, I also conducted research on some competitors to better grasp their strategies and processes.
